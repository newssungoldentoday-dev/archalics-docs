How Archa License Solves It


Archa License v1.0 introduces **Threshold + Fair Use + Delayed Open**.

The Model


.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1

   * - User Type
     - Commercial Use?
     - Obligation
   * - Individual / Indie / <100 employees & <$5M revenue
     - Yes, free
     - Attribution only (3.1)
   * - Threshold Entity (>100 emp or >$5M)
     - Yes, but conditional
     - Must do ONE of: commercial license, Sponsor, or Delayed Open (18 months)
   * - Big Tech Hosting as direct substitute
     - No, without agreement
     - Must get written agreement (3.3)

Key Innovations


1. Threshold Entity (Section 1.5)
   Clear numbers: 100 employees OR $5M revenue. No guessing.

2. Fair Use for Growth (Section 3.2)
   Small companies grow free. Once you scale, you give back.

3. Big Tech Protection (Section 3.3)
   Prevents strip-mining the project as a standalone hosted clone.

4. Delayed Open (Section 1.6 + 7)
   If Threshold Entity chooses option (iii), their mods become MIT after 18 months max. Knowledge returns to commons.

5. Patent Grant (Section 4)
   Same protection as Apache 2.0.

6. Low Liability Cap (Section 6)
   $100 cap for indie maintainers.

Comparison


* vs MIT: Archa adds sustainability at scale
* vs Apache 2.0: Archa adds Big Tech + Threshold protection
* vs SSPL / Commons Clause: Archa is NOT source-available trap — individuals remain fully free
* vs Fair Source: Archa has defined Threshold and auto MIT fallback via CHANGE_DATE

Result

A license that is:

* **Open** for 99% of developers
* **Fair** for growing companies
* **Protective** for maintainers
