Problem Statement


Why we built Archa License


Open source has a sustainability problem.

1. The Exploitation Problem
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
MIT / Apache projects get taken by Big Tech, hosted as a service, with $0 returned to maintainers.

Example: A 2-person library gets 10M downloads, AWS / Vercel clones it as a paid service.

2. The Growth Cliff
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
GPL protects against exploitation but scares startups and individuals.

* GPLv3: Too restrictive for commercial adoption
* MIT: Too permissive for maintainer protection

There was no middle ground.

3. The Maintenance Labor is Invisible
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
Thousands of hours of unpaid labor keep the commons alive. Licenses pretended this labor was free forever.

Our Position

Archa License Version 1.0 answers this:

* Free for individuals to learn, build, and share.
* Fair for growing businesses that benefit from the commons.
* Protected for maintainers against extractive use.

We don't want to kill open source. We want to make it survive.
